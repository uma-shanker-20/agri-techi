Wheat

Wheat is a type of cereal grain that is widely cultivated for its nutritional and economic value. It is a cool-season crop that thrives in temperate climates with adequate moisture.

Characteristics:

  • Scientific Name: Triticum aestivum
  • Family: Poaceae
  • Origin: Middle East
  • Cultivation: Worldwide

Nutritional Value:

Nutrient Value (per 100g)
Protein 10.5g
Fiber 2.7g
Carbohydrates 71.2g

Cultivation Practices:

  • Seeding: 200-300 seeds per square meter
  • Irrigation: 500-700 mm per year
  • Fertilization: NPK (10:10:10)
  • Pest Management: Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

Rice

Rice is a type of cereal grain that is widely cultivated for its nutritional and economic value. It is a warm-season crop that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with adequate moisture.

Characteristics:

  • Scientific Name: Oryza sativa
  • Family: Poaceae
  • Origin: Asia
  • Cultivation: Worldwide

Nutritional Value:

Nutrient Value (per 100g)
Protein 6.5g
Fiber 0.6g
Carbohydrates 77.2g

Cultivation Practices:

  • Seeding: 150-200 seeds per square meter
  • Irrigation: 1000-1500 mm per year
  • Fertilization: NPK (15:15:15)
  • Pest Management: Integrated Pest Management (IPM)
